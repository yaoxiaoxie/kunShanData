Section : Preparation Attachment :
Workloading : 5(Min) LeadingTime : 5(Min) Phase : Test Log Mandatory : N
Ensure image and BIOS are appropriate.
N
You can get audio files(noise/music) by Download Case Tools
N
Supported noise types for Voice Focus: Stationary background(fan, vacuum cleaner, driving car, …); Non-stationary background(cafeteria, street, airport, …); Transient(baby crying, dog barking, siren, …); Music
N
Limitations for Voice Focus: Strong background speech (e.g: side conversations) may not be suppressed fully
N
Enable hibernate function. Control panel->hardware and sound->power options->choose what the power buttons do->change settings that are currently unavailable
N
Section : Function Test Attachment :
Workloading : 35(Min) LeadingTime : 35(Min) Phase : Test Log Mandatory : N
1.Verify Default Audio Enhancement Setting Microsoft Windows Studio voice focus
N
2.Open Settings->system->sound->all sound devices->microphone array->audio enhancements
Default setting is 'Device Default Effects'
N
3.Open voice recorder app and start recording, Introduce some background noise such as typing, music, etc
Noise will be suppressed when playing the audio recording. Make sure smart noise cancelling is not off when recording.
N
4.If you change Audio Enhancement setting while recording is in progress, you will get warning(setting page) to highlight device is currently in use; may cause audio app issue.
N
5.Change Audio Enhancements to 'Off'
N
6.Open voice recorder app and start recording, Introduce some background noise such as typing, music, etc
Noise will not be suppressed when playing the audio recording
N
7.Change Audio Enhancements to 'Microsoft Windows Studio Voice Focus'
Turn on Voice Focus under the Audio Enhancements setting
N
8.Open voice recorder app and start recording, Introduce some background noise such as typing, music, etc
Noise will be suppressed when playing the audio recording
N
9.Verify Sound properties setting page load time when voice focus is on compared to when voice focus is off
Load time is normal, no delay
N
10.Turn on Automatic Framing, Background Effects, and Eye Contact
Settings->Bluetooth&devices->cameras->integrated camera->windows studio effects
N
11.Start Recording on Voice Recorder while camera effects is on
Make sure no impact to the time taken to apply the audio&camera effect, both audio and camera effects can work normally
N
Section : Stability Test Attachment :
Workloading : 55(Min) LeadingTime : 85(Min) Phase : Test Log Mandatory : N
1.Change Audio Enhancements to 'Microsoft Windows Studio Voice Focus'
Turn on Voice Focus under the Audio Enhancements setting
N
2.Start Recording on Voice Recorder 30 seconds
Check NPU utilisation in task manager.Ensure NPU utilisation is at a stable value during recording
N
3.Playing the audio recording
Total recording time is accurate, no increase or decrease.Ensure speech no discontinue/distortion/words lost. Ensure no strange noise or sound glitch.
N
4.Repeat the above steps 5 times
Check the results
N
5.Change Audio Enhancements to 'Device Default Effects'
Make sure smart noise cancelling is not off
N
6.Repeat step 2-4
Check the results
N
7.Start Recording on Voice Recorder 30 minutes, introduce some background noise such as typing, music, etc
Playing the audio recording, noise is suppressed all the time.
N
Section : Power Management Test Attachment :
Workloading : 15(Min) LeadingTime : 15(Min) Phase : Test Log Mandatory : N
1.Change Audio Enhancements to 'Microsoft Windows Studio Voice Focus'
Turn on Voice Focus under the Audio Enhancements setting
N
2.Put the system to Sleep(MS/S3)(Please cover lid close and start menu) & Hibernation & WarmBoot & ColdBoot and resume
Voice focus keep on after resume
N
3.Change voice focus to off
N
4.Put the system to Sleep(MS/S3)(Please cover lid close and start menu) & Hibernation & WarmBoot & ColdBoot and resume
Voice focus keep off after resume
N
